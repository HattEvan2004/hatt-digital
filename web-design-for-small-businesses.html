 Why it matters
    
A clear website makes a small business look like a safe bet.

    
Customers research before they buy or book. A simple, professional site tells them what you offer, why you’re trustworthy, and how to reach you — the three things that turn a stranger into a customer.

  

    
01
Look professional on any budget
A clean site makes a one-person shop look just as credible as the big competitor down the road.

    
02
Explain your offer clearly
Most visitors leave because they can’t quickly tell what you do. Clear messaging keeps them.

    
03
Own your online home
Unlike a social page, your website is yours — it ranks on Google and works the way you need it to.

  






  
 Where most fall short
    
Common problems with small business websites

    
The patterns that keep small business sites from pulling their weight.

  

    
01
Only a social media page
Facebook and Instagram are great, but they can’t rank on Google or fully explain your business.

    
02
DIY site that never got finished
A half-built builder site can do more harm than good when customers are deciding.

    
03
No clear call to action
If visitors don’t know the next step, they don’t take one.

    
04
Doesn’t work on phones
Most local customers are on mobile; a clunky phone experience sends them elsewhere.
