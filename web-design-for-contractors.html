 Why it matters
    
Contractors win work on trust. Your website should prove it.

    
People hiring a contractor are letting you into their home or onto their job site. Before they call, they want to feel sure you’re real, reliable, and local. A clear website does that work before the phone even rings.

  

    
01
Look established and legitimate
A clean, modern site signals a real business, not a one-off side gig. That’s often the difference between a call and a scroll-past.

    
02
Show the work and the service area
Clear services and the areas you cover help the right customers self-qualify before they reach out, so you get better-fit leads.

    
03
Make the next step obvious
A big, tappable “Get a quote” or “Call now” button means an interested homeowner doesn’t have to hunt for how to reach you.

  






  
 Where most fall short
    
Common problems with contractor websites

    
Most contractor sites aren’t bad on purpose, they just weren’t built to bring in work. Here’s what usually holds them back.

  

    
01
No website, just a Facebook page
A social page is fine, but it can’t rank on Google, lay out your services clearly, or look like an established company.

    
02
Hard to use on a phone
Most homeowners check you out on their phone. If the site is slow or fiddly to tap, they bounce to the next contractor.

    
03
Unclear services and areas
If a visitor can’t tell in five seconds what you do and where you work, they assume you’re not a fit and move on.

    
04
No obvious way to get a quote
Buried phone numbers and missing forms quietly cost you the leads that were ready to reach out.
